Abstract

The automated inspection of submerged marine infrastructure is critical for predictive maintenance yet remains hindered by hostile optical conditions and data scarcity. Despite the considerable advancement of deep learning in terrestrial defect detection, the direct application of large-scale models in the underwater domain typically fails due to severe domain shifts caused by turbidity and light attenuation. The present study evaluates YOLO26 architectures for the purpose of underwater corrosion detection, comparing three complementary strategies: model scaling (Nano, Medium, Large), multi-model fusion via Weighted Box Fusion (WBF), and robust inference via Test-Time Augmentation (TTA). Our experiments on the SINTEF LIACI dataset (the first Object Detection baseline reported on this repository) reveal that the Medium variant (20.4M parameters) trained via Direct Transfer Learning achieves the strongest individual performance (F1-Score: 0.77, mAP@50: 0.82), while Nano and Large models suffer from insufficient capacity and overfitting, respectively. Although WBF ensembling improves recall and mAP@50 over single-pass inference, test-time augmentation applied to the medium backbone remains the strongest inference strategy. It achieves an mAP@50 of 0.75 and a recall of 0.79 under a unified fixed-threshold evaluation and demonstrates robust generalisation, improving recall by +6.28% on an independent TrashCan benchmark. The model was retrained on this benchmark via direct transfer as a methodological verification. A post-detection refinement stage using the Foundation Model SAM 3 provides an independent, zero-shot morphological characterization of the detected regions. With workstation-level inference latency compatible with offline or low-rate inspection workflows, the proposed framework offers a viable solution for resource-constrained inspection-class Remotely Operated Vehicles (ROVs).

Proposal

A two-stage, reproducible pipeline for underwater corrosion assessment.

We establish the first standardized Object Detection baseline on the SINTEF LIACI underwater inspection dataset by converting its pixel-level masks into bounding-box annotations. A YOLO26 (Medium) detector, trained via Direct Transfer, localizes corrosion, and its boxes are used as spatial prompts for a zero-shot SAM 3 stage that recovers pixel-level morphology.   • Model scaling (Nano / Medium / Large). The Medium variant (20.4M params) is the optimal trade-off; Nano underfits and Large overfits environmental noise.
  • TTA > WBF. Single-model Test-Time Augmentation (F1 0.72) consistently outperforms multi-model Weighted Box Fusion (F1 0.68) — geometric diversity at inference beats model fusion while reducing computational overhead.
  • External verification. The TTA-driven gains transfer to the independent TrashCan benchmark when the model is retrained via Direct Transfer.
  • Morphological refinement. A zero-shot SAM 3 stage recovers the detected corrosion regions at the pixel level, validated against ground-truth masks.

Reproducibility

Every quantitative result is produced by a notebook published with its executed outputs.

  • Executed notebooks — one per experiment (dataset prep, training, model comparison, WBF, TTA/S-TTA, TrashCan, SAM 3).
  • Shared utilities — seeding, split loading, and the single COCO-compatible metric.
  • Frozen splits — the exact held-out 33-image LIACI test set.
  • Pinned environment — ultralytics==8.4.5 (critical pin), torch==2.8.0, SAM 3 via transformers.

Data availability

The code, executed notebooks, and frozen evaluation splits supporting this study are openly available in the accompanying repository. The underlying datasets are third-party and publicly accessible from their original sources: the SINTEF LIACI underwater inspection dataset, the TrashCan 1.0 dataset (cross-domain verification), and an aerial corrosion dataset hosted on Roboflow (sequential-transfer pre-training); the corresponding download routines are included in the released notebooks. Trained model checkpoints will be deposited in a DOI-referenced archive upon acceptance.
